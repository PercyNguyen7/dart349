Research Essay

The Racist Algorithm

Research

Within two days of the recent acquisition of Twitter by business magnate Elon Musk, trolls stormed the platform with racist tweets. The use of the n word rose by 500% in the span of 12 hours after the event (Duffy). These distasteful practices of trollings along with real cyber-racism has encouraged me to create an accessible web-page project that condemns these practices.

This Racist Algorithm originates as a research-creation project in CART410. The research objective is to find common patterns of cyber-racism as well as to understand its impact on the current CART students.

Research Methodology 1:

I created a new undercover Twitter account to further understand how the algorithm caters to a racist account. Over a course of four hours, I actively engage with racist accounts on the platform. I then bookmark the ten most popular racist tweets, explain what makes it racist in one sentence while recording my feelings about it in another sentence.

Research Methodology 2:

I now conduct interviews with CART students to question their past experience with racism in the past 5 years as well as ask them to react to the top ten list of racist tweets gathered in the first procedure.

The Artistic Project

I decide to use satire to ridicule the problem while also confronting and raising awareness of the issue. It merges both fiction and nonfiction through an interactive, speculative narrative that denounces real world proof of hate speech on the platform.

The Racist Algorithm is a web-based roleplay satirical game where users play as Twitter’s algorithm. Users are first welcomed to the game as Algorithm 1488, one of Twitter’s abundant algorithm systems. Algorithms are tasked with increasing Twitter user engagement in order to grow the platform’s revenue through advertising. Algorithm 1488 is assigned user “Trevor”, a new Twitter account that engages with content under the #whitelivesmatter and #racialjokes. An RM Bar, stands for the Revenue Meter and Racist Meter, measures Twitter's total revenue made from advertisements to Trevor. It increases for every positive interaction that Trevor creates with the platform.

The game extends over ten days. At the beginning of each day, players are offered three tweets which they must choose one to recommend to Trevor. All three tweets emerge from different topics with one of them illustrating racism. With racism as one of Trevor’s strongest interests, players may only recommend the racist Tweet. Players are given the illusion of choice. Failure to recommend the racist tweet only prompts a sarcastic message to remind the player of Trevor’s interests in racism and Twitter’s goal of maximizing profit.

As more racist contents engage with his daily recommended racist tweets, Trevor portrays his racist nature by tweeting increasingly xenophobics content on his profile. Trevor's personal data and latest activities are also updated everyday. Trevor’s depiction of racism is a combination of four CART students’ various experience with racial discrimination.

The game ends after the end of the tenth day with Trevor tweeting an image of himself in front of the US Capitol, ready to redeem justice for his fellow patriots.

Purpose

The Racist Algorithm critiques the flaw of AI Algorithms in social media as they follow their task blindly while lacking the human intelligence to recognize and block racist content. A short description of the real purpose of the game will be available at the end of the experience.

Censoring racist slurs can be met with different spelling of the word or implicit racism. Racism could also be disguised as memes of racial inferiority ideology that remain protected under the guise of dark humor. Yet the joke is on the victims of the jokes. Is it humane and appropriate to make absurd jokes that demean an ethnicity? What does this speak of the core values of our society if we were to exploit freedom of speech for internet popularity? Furthermore, the practices of making and circulating racial memes not only damage the ethnicity in question but also reinforce “a racist digital fellowship among meme-enthusiasts” (Al-Natour, par.conclusion).

This project also begs the question: “Must one set limits to freedom of speech on social media?”. Or may freedom of speech and self-expression exist without any boundaries and limits despite its dissemination of hate speech and hate crime?

Ethics Statement

A trigger warning will be mentioned at the beginning of the game to warn users about the hurtful content in the website.

As the piece harshly critiques the racist nature of over ten Twitter accounts, I also remain anonymous. The game also encourages players to not send hate to any of the racist accounts, but rather educate the younger generation to not repeat the same problem.
